Fluid Mechanics Notes

Introduction

Fluid Mechanics studies the behavior of fluids (liquids and gases) at rest and in motion. It is essential for designing hydraulic structures and understanding fluid flow in civil engineering.

Key Topics

  • Fluid Properties: Density, viscosity, and surface tension.
  • Hydrostatics: Pressure, buoyancy, and manometry.
  • Fluid Dynamics: Bernoulli’s equation and continuity equation.
  • Flow in Pipes: Laminar and turbulent flow, head loss.
  • Open Channel Flow: Manning’s equation and channel design.
